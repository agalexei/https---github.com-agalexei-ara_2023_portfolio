My

Process

01 — Direction

First of all, what I do its Research.
I try to get as much information about the product, industry, competitors and who are your main users; their pain points (problems), as well as the main tasks of the product.
I then come up with a plan based on these findings, the needs and insights gathered from research. I define objectives and subsidiary goals.

02 — Ux/Ui

I define ideas worth refining based on my learnings from the research and create moodboards, system maps and wireframe mockups. This helps better visualise what the product will look like and tweak as needed.
I build a representation of all ideas and solutions. Before working on the final product I create prototypes to document the experience and interactive prototypes to test them with your real users.

03 — Design

The product should be approved by you and users through user testing sessions. I ask users test my prototype and leave feedback. After that, I work to improve the product based on the knowledge and what I learnt from testing.
Designs are meant to be aesthetic and at the same time easy to use. So I create the design as per the users' requirement, that is both user-friendly and easy to use.

Me

I started my UX design journey back in 2020, while the world as we knew it went into isolation and changed in an instant. People started connecting with each other through technology more than ever, and I found this to be a great opportunity to have a career change into UX design, which I have no regrets doing. UX allows me to combine my creative passions with my professional experience and I feel privileged to be able to be able to work in an industry that I am so passionate about.

Since then, I and have learnt a lot of the processes, concepts and theories of UX/UI and product design from my own professional experiences as well as through attending workshops, reading blogs and applying these learnings to my work. I am very much focused on the details and goal oriented. I enjoy working on products end to end, from ideation all the way to development.
