Best & Less Gift Registry

Best & Less is an Australian national retailer, operating in almost 200 store locations and online, with one of the biggest offerings of baby products nationally.
Process
I was the sole UX/UI designer on this project. It was one of my first commercial projects where I took full ownership of the design process. I worked closely with the optimisation and e-commerce teams, as well as front-end developers to ensure the final product fulfilled the clients' needs.
Mobile viewport.
The Problem
With Best & Less' wide range of products and established national presence, how can we increase market share by adding additional services to our client base, while also gain new customers?
The Solution
We believe a new offering of a baby shower gift registry will provide a new service to online customers, improving their online shopping experience as well as generate new customers.
Research
Initial research included a competitor analysis where I looked at strengths and weaknesses of main competitors, as well as opportunities of improvement for Best & Less. My research also influenced the building of a user flow and journey.
Findings
BabyBunting's User Flow was straight forward. However there were a lot of areas of improvmement in terms of design. It was difficult to find how to navigate to the gift registry was, and 'Progress Steps' steps looked more like buttons. There was also no CTA or prompt where to start shopping. We found an opportunity for Best & Less to generate unique URLs and IDs for gift registries, in order to be shared with purchasers.

We found David Jones' Gift Registry to be stronger than BabyBunting's Gift Registry. David Jones allows users to create multiple Gift Registries, users are allowed to include co-registrants and products can be delivered straight to registrants' address. David Jones also offered discounts for users to purchase items that were not purchased on their gift registry.

Iteration:
We added a create new registry function on the gift registry page, to create additional registries. 'Create a Registry' form was simplified and number of steps were taken out to reduce user confusion Copy of buttons were changed from 'Discover Now' to 'Add Gifts to Registry' to help prompt the next step to users when following the journey. Information required was simplified and changed for 'Find a Registry', to address privacy concerns. Other subtle UI changes such as confirmation notifications changing from a more 'alert' red/pink colour to a 'positive' green.
