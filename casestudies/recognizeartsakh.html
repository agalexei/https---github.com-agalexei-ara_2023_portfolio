Recognize Artsakh

Recognize Artsakh is an online resource created to help organise the Armenian/Artsakh cause.

In 2020, the ethnic Armenian population of Nagorno-Karabakh (also known as Artsakh) were indiscriminantly attacked by shelled and attacked by external forces, denying people's right to self determination. The Recognize Artsakh project was an initiative I undertook because it is a cause I hold deep in my heart, as an Armenian. I saw the need to create a resource as we did not have any tools to help organise the cause, spread awareness as well as provide counterarguments to the oppressor's myths/talking points.
Process
As the creator and designer of the resource, I drew inspiration from decolonizepalestine.com. An online resource used to spread awarness for the Palestinian cause. I collaborated with Armenian advocates to help write the content for the website, as well as Armenian graphic designers and artists to help with the graphics on the pages, again further instilling this idea of community as the main purpose for the project.
The Problem
The Armenian community, worldwide does not have an online resource which helps organise us all in one place.
The Solution
Build a website to be used as a resource that helps raise awareness, spread our message and support the Armenian people in Artsakh who are currently facing immense challenges and difficulties, all in one place.
Research
Initial research included a competitor analysis where I looked at strengths and weaknesses of Decolonise Palestine. I also conducted guerilla and AB testing with Armenian peers to identify pain points and areas of improvement for Recognize Artsakh.
Problem statement:
"The Armenian cause is one and united. However, we have observed that Armenian acticvists and advocates do not have any one resource where all relevant and useful information can be foundd, which is causing some divisions within the community. How might we improve the user’s experience to provide a resource where all the information they would require can be found, all in one place?"
Iteration:
Some pages were quite long with the vast amounts of information. Information had to be refindded and UI elements introduced such as accordions to make reading the information more palettable.
Personal relection:
This project is very dear to me, and I am very happy to have spent my time working on it. I am sure this will be a valuable resource for armenians across the world. I see myself as an advocate for the Armenian cause and am proud to have used my professional expertise to help champion it, spread awareness and unify activists/advocates globally.
